Local Foods

Kerak Telor

Often called the "Betawi omelette," Kerak Telor is a legendary snack from Jakarta. It's made from sticky rice cooked with chicken or duck eggs, then topped with fried shallots, shredded coconut, and dried shrimp. Cooked over charcoal, it has a unique crispy texture and a savory, slightly sweet flavor that truly represents Jakarta's culinary heritage.

Soto Betawi

Soto Betawi is a rich and creamy beef soup, and a true comfort food for Jakartans. Unlike other Indonesian sotos, its broth is made with coconut milk (or sometimes fresh milk), giving it a distinctive, hearty flavor. It's filled with chunks of beef, offal, potatoes, and tomatoes, and usually served with rice, pickles, and a dash of lime.

Gado-Gado

Gado-gado is an Indonesian salad of steamed vegetables, hard-boiled eggs, tofu, tempeh, and lontong (rice cakes), all drenched in a flavorful peanut sauce dressing. While found across Indonesia, the Jakarta version is famous for its perfectly balanced sweet, savory, and slightly spicy sauce, making it a healthy and delicious meal.
