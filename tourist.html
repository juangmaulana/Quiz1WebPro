Tourist Destinations

National Monument (Monas)

The National Monument, or Monas, is the most iconic landmark of Jakarta, standing 132 meters tall in the center of Merdeka Square. It symbolizes the fight for Indonesia's independence. Visitors can take an elevator to the observation deck at the top for a stunning 360-degree view of the city skyline. The base of the monument houses a museum displaying dioramas of Indonesian history.

Kota Tua (Old Town)

Kota Tua, the "Old Town" of Jakarta, is a historic district that was once the heart of the Dutch colonial empire in Asia. Today, you can walk or bike around Fatahillah Square, surrounded by beautifully preserved colonial-era buildings that now serve as museums, cafes, and art galleries. It's a perfect place to step back in time and experience the city's rich history.

Ancol Dreamland

Ancol Dreamland is a massive integrated resort and amusement park located on Jakarta's waterfront. It's the city's premier destination for family fun and recreation. Ancol features Dunia Fantasi (Dufan) theme park, Sea World, Atlantis Water Adventure, beaches, a marina, and an art market (Pasar Seni). It's the perfect escape from the city's hustle and bustle.

Istiqlal Mosque

Istiqlal Mosque is the largest mosque in Southeast Asia and a stunning architectural marvel. Its name, "Istiqlal," is Arabic for "independence," commemorating Indonesia's freedom. The mosque stands as a powerful symbol of religious tolerance, located directly across from the Jakarta Cathedral. Its vast prayer hall, minimalist design, and massive dome are truly awe-inspiring, welcoming visitors from all faiths to appreciate its serene and grand atmosphere.
